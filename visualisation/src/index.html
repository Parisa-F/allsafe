Do you know how fast the scam growth in the past few years?

From January 2020 to July 2024, there have been 60,902 reported social media scams affecting young adults in Australia. Scams began to rise noticeably at the end of 2022, and more than 1,200 scams were reported in each of the following months.

When comparing 2023 to 2022, reported scams increased by about 30%. The number of scams reported between January and July 2024 has already risen by 49% compared to the same period in 2022.

Does the money lost follow a similar pattern as the number of reports?

While the top 5 most frequent scams align with the top 5 scams causing the most financial loss, their patterns have some differences.
In 2024, the top 5 scams alone accounted for approximately AUD 31,245,435 in losses, representing about 96% of the total amount lost on social media scams with young adults. Among these, investment scams have the highest financial impact, making up 60% of the total losses, followed by dating and romance scams, which account for around 20%.
The different patterns from the number of reports and money loss indicate that no matter which type of scam you are facing, the potential loss can accumulate into a vast amount and continuously influence our daily lives.
